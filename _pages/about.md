I am a researcher in physics specializing in nonlinear vibration energy harvesting. My work focuses on harvesting energy from flow-induced vibrations such as galloping and vortex-induced vibrations.

I use perturbation methods and scientific programming tools to analyze and simulate nonlinear dynamical systems, with the goal of improving the efficiency of energy harvesting systems.

My research interests include nonlinear dynamics, flow-induced vibrations, perturbation methods, stability analysis, and mathematical modeling.

I aim to develop efficient and innovative solutions for energy harvesting from ambient vibrations and to contribute to practical engineering applications.
